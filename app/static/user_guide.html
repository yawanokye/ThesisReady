Module-by-module guidance

Go directly to the stage that matches your work

1

Before topic approval

Topic Ideas

Generate and compare researchable titles, synopses, objectives, methodology directions, constructs and feasibility notes.

Required to run

  • Research area or broad topic
  • Context, sector or population
  • Country or institutional setting
  • Academic level

Strongly recommended

  • Keywords or constructs
  • Preferred methodology
  • Data-access status
  • Supervisor feasibility check

Optional or conditional

  • Trend focus
  • Older foundational sources
  • Paid idea count
  • Payment email for an unlocked set

2

After topic approval

Thesis Workspace

Develop an editable chapter working draft from an approved topic, research decisions, evidence, sources, results and school requirements.

Required to run

  • Project title and academic level
  • Thesis, dissertation or project format
  • Data source and analytical orientation
  • Research area, study context and chapter
  • Selected chapter sections and essential guided details

Strongly recommended

  • Specific objectives and research questions
  • Relevant sources and evidence anchors
  • School-specific format requirements
  • Supervisor comments and approved methodology

Optional or conditional

  • Custom page range
  • Writing-style sample and phrases to avoid
  • Previous chapters for alignment
  • Uploaded results for results-based chapters

3

Revision stage

Chapter Strengthener

Revise an existing chapter while preserving approved research decisions, evidence, data and findings.

Required to run

  • Thesis or dissertation title
  • Academic level and chapter
  • Revision source and output scope
  • Existing chapter text or uploaded chapter
  • Authorship and responsibility confirmation

Strongly recommended

  • Objectives, questions, theory and variables
  • Approved methodology and confirmed results
  • Specific strengthening direction
  • Supervisor comments requiring action

Optional or conditional

  • Previous chapters or full thesis for alignment
  • Selected-section output
  • Custom target page range
  • New custom section and source search

Before relying on any output

Complete the final academic checks

AlignmentConfirm that the title, problem, purpose, objectives, questions, variables and methods agree.
EvidenceVerify every factual claim, statistic, result, citation and reference independently.
FeasibilityConfirm that the proposed data, instrument, sample and analysis are realistic and accessible.
Institutional fitMatch headings, page range, style and assessment requirements to the school format.
Human reviewReview and revise the working draft with the student and supervisor before submission.
Clean startUse Clear and start new job before moving to a different study or chapter.
